**Podcast library** is a free website for discovering shows and listening in your browser. This page explains how to get the most out of it, what is stored on your device, and where to find daily and curated collections.

## Browse and search

1. Open **[All shows](/shows)** for the full A–Z directory. You can sort A–Z, Z–A, or shuffle, and use pagination for large lists.
2. Use the **search box in the header** to filter by title (and related text) as you type.
3. Open **[Categories](/category)** for themed collections—fiction, sleep-friendly audio, daily-updating shows, and more.

## Play an episode

1. Open any **show page**. Episodes load from that show’s public RSS feed.
2. Tap the **circular play control** on an episode row. Audio plays in the **bottom player**, which stays visible as you navigate.
3. Use **pause**, **seek**, and **skip** controls on the dock. Playback continues while you browse other pages on the same site.

## Favorites and “Recently listened”

- **Favorites:** On a show page, use the heart on the cover to save a show. View them under **[Favorites](/favorites)**. Favorites are stored **only in this browser**.
- **Recently listened:** Episodes you play appear under **[Recently listened](/recent)** with an approximate **progress** bar. This is also **local to the device**—it does not create an account or sync to the cloud.

If you switch browsers or clear site data, favorites and progress reset.

## Daily and “recently updated” picks

The **[Daily](/category/daily)** category highlights shows that publish on a regular cadence. On the **home page**, you may also see a **“Recently updated (Daily)”** row: those are the same Daily collection shows **re-ordered by the newest activity in each RSS feed** (refreshed periodically), so return visitors can spot feeds that just published.

## Limitations (honest scope)

- **No accounts:** There is no login; convenience features rely on browser storage.
- **Feeds can change:** Publishers control RSS; a missing episode or broken URL is usually on the feed or host side.
- **Not a podcast host:** The site does not upload or replace your files; it points to URLs in each feed.
