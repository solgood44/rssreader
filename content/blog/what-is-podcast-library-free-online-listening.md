If you searched for a **free online podcast library** and landed here, you are in the right place. **Podcast library** is a website—not an app store download—where you can browse shows, open episode lists, and press play in your browser.

## What you get here

Think of it as a **catalog plus a simple web player**:

- **Browse** hundreds of shows sorted A–Z or by [category](/category).
- **Search** by title from the header as you type.
- **Play** episodes in a persistent player at the bottom of the screen while you keep browsing.
- **Save favorites** and see **recently listened** episodes on this device only—no login required.

Audio streams from each show’s public RSS feed. The site does not host or replace those files; it organizes feeds and makes them easy to open on the web.

## Who it is for

Podcast library works well if you:

- Want to **listen without installing another app**
- Like **classic audiobooks, daily story feeds, sleep sounds, or old-time radio** in one place
- Prefer a **calm, browse-first** experience over algorithm-heavy feeds
- Need a **free** option for background listening at home or on a laptop

It is less ideal if you need offline downloads, cross-device sync, or a social feed. Those features usually live in native podcast apps with accounts.

## How it is organized

Shows are grouped the same way podcast directories tag them—**[Fiction](/category/fiction)**, **[Books](/category/books)**, **[Daily](/category/daily)** (feeds that publish on a regular cadence), **[Performing Arts](/category/performing-arts)** (including classic radio), and more. The [home page](/) highlights daily picks and a sample of the wider catalog; **[All shows](/shows)** is the full directory.

## A quick note on privacy and progress

Favorites, playback position, and your recently played list are stored **in this browser only** (local storage). That keeps things simple and free, but clearing site data or switching browsers resets your history. More detail is on **[About](/about)**.
