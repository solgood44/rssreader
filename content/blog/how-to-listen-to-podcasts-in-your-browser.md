You do not need Apple Podcasts, Spotify, or any other app to listen here. **Podcast library** runs in your browser like a lightweight podcast client. Here is the whole workflow in plain language.

## 1. Find a show

Three easy paths:

- **[All shows](/shows)** — full A–Z directory with sort and pagination.
- **[Categories](/category)** — themed collections (fiction, daily feeds, books, and more).
- **Header search** — type a title; results filter as you go.

The [home page](/) also surfaces **Daily** picks and a **Recently updated (Daily)** row when feeds have fresh episodes.

## 2. Open episodes and press play

1. Click any show to open its page.
2. Episodes load from that show’s public RSS feed.
3. Tap the **play button** on an episode row.
4. Audio plays in the **bottom player**, which stays visible as you navigate other pages on the site.

Use pause, seek, and skip on the dock. As long as the tab stays open, you can browse while you listen.

## 3. Save favorites

On a show page, tap the **heart on the cover** to favorite that show. Open **[Favorites](/favorites)** anytime to jump back. Favorites live **only in this browser**—there is no cloud account.

## 4. Resume where you left off

Episodes you play show up under **[Recently listened](/recent)** with an approximate progress bar. Handy when you pause mid-chapter and come back later on the same device.

If you clear site data, use private browsing, or switch browsers, favorites and progress reset. That is the tradeoff for not requiring a login.

## 5. Tips that actually help

- **Phone or tablet:** Add the site to your home screen if your browser supports it; playback still runs in the browser.
- **Sleep listening:** Browse **[Daily](/category/daily)** or ambient and relaxation shows in categories like **[Mental Health](/category/mental-health)**—many listeners use long rain or story feeds overnight.
- **Broken episode?** Publishers control RSS URLs. If one link fails, try another episode or check back later.

## What this site does not do

- No sign-up or sync across phones and laptops
- No offline downloads
- No uploading or editing feeds (it reads public RSS only)
