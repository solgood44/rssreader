## What Exactly is Green Noise?

Green noise is environmental audio that contains a frequency which possesses calming natural characteristics. Unlike white noise, green noise puts emphasis on mid-range frequencies. Green noise sits between white noise and brown noise on the sound spectrum, with brown noise having more energy in the lower frequencies and resembling a deep rumble or waterfall. When listening to it you might imagine a leafy forest in the distance with leaves blowing in the wind or even a faint stream rushing by. Green noise also sits between white noise and pink noise, with pink noise having more energy in the lower and middle frequencies and promoting relaxation and tranquility. The noise is named after the ‘green’ frequencies which dominate in the audio and produce a very organic and relaxing sound.

### What Does Green Noise Sound Like?

Where white noise mixes all frequencies together, green noise filters and boosts the volume of natural sounds primarily around 500 Hz, which naturally sound more balanced and pleasing to the ear. Examples of these pleasing sounds are leaves blowing in the wind, buzzing insects or a small stream rushing by; some might call it the music of nature. Green noise includes nature sounds like rustling leaves or rain, which can aid in relaxation and unwinding.
